"Ready to add more information? Today, we learn how to say **'Also'** and how to connect **'Nouns'**."

Logic: Replacing the Topic Marker

は (wa)
も (mo)

Sentence 1

田中さん は 日本人 です。

Tanaka-san is Japanese.

Sentence 2 (Addition)

鈴木さん も 日本人 です。

Suzuki-san is **also** Japanese.

Grammar Rule

When you want to say "Also" or "Too", you **must replace** the particle は (wa) with も (mo). Never say "wa mo" together!

Connecting Two Nouns / 名詞の接続

Owner (N1)
の
Item (N2)

Example 1: Possession

わたし の 車

Watashi no kuruma (My car / My item)

Example 2: Attribute/Belonging

日本 の カメラ

Nihon no kamera (Japanese camera / Camera of Japan)

Think of の (no) as a "Glue". It connects two nouns to show ownership, origin, or category. In English, it's often similar to 's or of.

Let's build a complex sentence logically:

1. Basic

これ は 本 です。

"This is a book."

2. Adding Owner (no)

これ は わたし の 本 です。

"This is my book."

3. Adding Another Person (mo)

それ も わたし の 本 です。

"That is also my book."
