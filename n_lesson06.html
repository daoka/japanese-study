The Golden Rule

In English, verbs come in the middle: "I eat an apple."
In Japanese, verbs ALWAYS come at the END of the sentence.

To Eat

たべます

(Tabemasu)

To Drink

のみます

(Nomimasu)

To Wake up

おきます

(Okimasu)

To Sleep

ねます

(Nemasu)

To say "eat an apple" or "drink water", we need a new glue (particle) to connect the Object to the Verb.

を

The Object Marker (wo)

Written as "wo" (を), but pronounced as "o".

Sentence Builder

Object
を (o)
Verb ます。

パン を たべます。

Pan o tabemasu (I eat bread).

みず を のみます。

Mizu o nomimasu (I drink water).

O'clock

〜時 (ji)

  • 6時 (Roku-ji)
  • 7時 (Shichi-ji)
  • 8時 (Hachi-ji)
  • 9時 (Ku-ji)
に

The Time Marker (ni)

Like "at" or "on" in English. Attach it directly to the specific time.

6時 に おきます。

Roku-ji ni okimasu (I wake up at 6:00).

Translate the routine (Hover to Reveal):

"I eat sushi at 8 o'clock."
(8 o'clock = 8時 / Sushi = すし)

Reveal Answer
わたし は 8時 に すし を たべます。

"I sleep at 11 o'clock."
(11 o'clock = 11時)

Reveal Answer
わたし は 11時 に ねます。

*No object (を) is needed for sleeping!
