Identify the correct particle

"I buy sushi at the supermarket."

スーパー ( ? ) すし を かいます。

スーパー で すし を かいます。

Because the supermarket is the "Action Theater"!

These two particles work together to create boundaries in Time and Space.

Start Point

から

(kara / from)

End Point

まで

(made / to, until)

Logic of Time

9じ から 5じ まで です。

"It is from 9:00 to 5:00."

Logic of Space

とうきょう から おおさか まで。

"From Tokyo to Osaka."

The "Spotlight" Particle: が (ga)

While は (wa) sets the general topic, が (ga) acts as a spotlight identifying a specific subject. It is heavily used with words like "Like" and "Understand".

Formula: [Topic] wa [Subject] ga [Adjective/Verb]

わたし は すし が すき です。

Literal translation: "As for me (wa), Sushi is the thing (ga) I like."

Natural English: "I like sushi."

Crucial Logic

In Japanese, "Like" (Suki) is NOT an action verb. It is an adjective. Therefore, you never use the object marker を (o). You must use が (ga)!

These particles are placed at the very end of the sentence (after desu or masu) to add conversational vibe.

ね (ne)

The "Agreement" Seeker

Like saying "...isn't it?" or "...right?"

いい てんき ですね。

It's good weather, isn't it?

よ (yo)

The "Informer"

Like saying "I tell you!" or giving new info.

この ほん は いい ですよ。

This book is good, I tell you!
